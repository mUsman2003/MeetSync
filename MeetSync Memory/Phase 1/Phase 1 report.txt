Phase 1: Problem Identification & Experience Research

1.1

1. The Problem (Core Focus)

In high-velocity small teams and digital classrooms, there is a growing gap between attendance
and engagement—a phenomenon we call ”Ghosting in Plain Sight.” The core of the
problem is twofold:
• Decision Amnesia: Critical tasks assigned ”on the fly” are lost because there is no
real-time bridge between verbal intent and documented action.
• Participation Decay: In remote environments, it is easy for participants to join a session and physically or mentally disappear. Without a real-time feedback loop, meeting
leads and educators cannot distinguish between a ”connected” user and a ”participating” one.
1.2

2. Who Has This Problem?
• Primary Users: Project Leads & Scrum Masters (chasing verbal commitments) and
Educators & Online Instructors (struggling to measure comprehension in real-time).
• Secondary Stakeholders: Individual Team Members (rework due to missed context)
and Students (falling behind when lack of participation is unaddressed).

1

1.3

3. Current Behavior & Workarounds
• The ”Camera On” Rule: A blunt, intrusive workaround that measures physical
presence but not mental engagement.
• Manual Scribing: Error-prone and misses the ”engagement” level of other attendees.
• Passive Attendance Logs: Zoom/Teams logs show ”joined” status but not if the
user ”disappeared” mentally while the tab was open.

1.4

4. Existing Products or Alternatives
• Otter.ai / Fireflies.ai: Passive tools. They capture what was said but not how well
it was received or who was actually paying attention.
• Standard LMS (Canvas/Moodle): These track ”submission” but not the ”engagement” during live instruction, creating a lag in feedback.

2. Commercial Viability Assessment
• Target Buyer vs. User: A significant hurdle exists where the User (Developer) may
feel monitored, while the Buyer (CTO/PM) sees the value in efficiency.
• Differentiation: MeetSync is Active, whereas current leaders are Passive.
• AI Specific Risks: Hallucinations where AI ”invents” tasks, and Latency where
quizzes arrive too late to be relevant.

2.3

3. Elaborative SMART Analysis of Business Goals

A. Objective (Quantitative) Goals
• Reduce Follow-up Meetings:
Specific: Target a 30% reduction in ”clarification” syncs.
Measurable: Measured by calendar metadata before and after adoption.
Achievable: Achievable if task extraction accuracy exceeds 90%.
Relevant: Directly addresses the ”Time Waste” impact area.
Time-bound: Evaluate over the first 3 months of deployment.
• Achieve $5,000 MRR:
Specific: Reach $5k in Monthly Recurring Revenue.
Measurable: Tracked through Stripe/Subscription dashboard.
Achievable: High risk due to API costs; requires 500 paid users.
Relevant: Essential for financial sustainability.
Time-bound: 12 months post-launch.
• Task Extraction Accuracy:
Specific: Maintain a 95% F1-score for intent extraction.
3

Measurable: Measured via manual audit of 10% of calls.
Achievable: Challenging due to ”Hallucination” risks in AI.
Relevant: Critical for user trust and retention.
Time-bound: Continuous monitoring; monthly audits.
B. Subjective (Qualitative) Goals
• Build User Trust:
Specific: Users feel confident that ”verbal = documented.”
Measurable: Measured via Qualitative Net Promoter Score (NPS).
Achievable: Possible with high accuracy and low latency.
Relevant: Essential to overcome the ”Privacy Barrier.”
Time-bound: Bi-annual survey cycles.
• Reduce Meeting Stress:
Specific: Lower the perceived stress of ”missing something.”
Measurable: Pre- and Post-user perception interviews.
Achievable: Achievable for Project Leads; harder for audited Devs.
Relevant: Addresses the ”Stress” cost identified in Phase 0.
Time-bound: 6 months post-onboarding.
• Perceived Reliability:
Specific: Tool is seen as a ”Black Box” source of truth.
Measurable: Measured by frequency of users ”citing” the tool in Slack.
Achievable: Depends entirely on consistent uptime and accuracy.
Relevant: Aligns with UVP of ”Actionable Clarity.”
Time-bound: 6 months post-launch.
