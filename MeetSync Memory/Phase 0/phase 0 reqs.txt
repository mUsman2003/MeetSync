AI Product Development
Course Project — Phase 0
Problem & Product Framing (Discovery Gate)
Status: Mandatory
Grading: Approval / Revision Required (no numeric score)
Duration: 5–9 days
Submission format: Short written artifact or short presentation


Purpose of Phase 0
Phase 0 exists to ensure that only real problems enter the course project.
Before you analyze business viability, conduct research, or build an MVP, you must demonstrate that:
* A genuine problem exists
* Real people experience it
* It is worth examining as a potential product
This phase deliberately precedes any business modeling or technical planning.
If the problem is unclear, everything that follows will fail.
What Phase 0 Is — and Is Not
Phase 0 is
* Exploratory
* Qualitative
* Problem-focused
* Lightweight but thoughtful
Phase 0 is not
* A business plan
* A pitch deck
* A solution proposal
* A technical design exercise


What You Must Submit
Each team must submit one Phase 0 artifact in any one of the formats below.
Allowed Formats (choose one)
1. Short written document (1–2 pages, PDF or Word)
2. Short presentation (5–6 slides)
3. Brief video (3–4 minutes, explaining the idea)
4. Existing product reference
   * Name + link to a product you want to:
      * Improve meaningfully, or
      * Rebuild for a different market or context
Regardless of format, the content requirements are the same.


Required Content (All Must Be Included)
1. The Problem (Core Focus)
Clearly describe:
* What the problem is
* When and where it occurs
* Why it is frustrating, risky, or inefficient
Focus on experience, not solutions.
Avoid phrases like:
* “We will build…”
* “Our AI system…”
* “The solution is…”


2. Who Has This Problem?
Be specific.
Identify:
* Primary users (who feels the pain directly)
* Secondary stakeholders (who are affected indirectly)
Generic labels such as “users” or “companies” are insufficient.
________________


3. Current Behavior & Workarounds
Explain how people currently deal with this problem, including:
* Tools they use
* Manual processes
* Informal workarounds
* Why these are unsatisfying
This section proves the problem is real and recurring.


4. Why This Problem Matters
Explain:
* What happens if the problem is not solved
* Who pays the cost (time, money, risk, stress, inefficiency)
* Why this is worth addressing now
You are arguing for problem importance, not product greatness.


5. Existing Products or Alternatives (Light)
If relevant:
* Name 1–3 existing products or solutions
* Briefly state what they do
* Explain what they do not solve well
This is not competitive analysis — that comes later.
6. High-Level Product Intuition (Brief)
You may include a one-paragraph intuition about:
* What kind of product might address the problem
Rules:
* Keep this under 10% of your submission
* No technical details
* No feature lists
* No architecture discussion


Evidence Expectations
You are expected to do light validation, such as:
* Informal conversations
* Public complaints, forums, reviews
* Articles or reports
* Observations from real contexts
You must be able to answer:
“How do you know this problem exists?”
Evaluation Criteria (Approval Gate)
Your submission will be reviewed against the following criteria.
Approval Criteria
* The problem is clearly and concretely defined
* Real users and contexts are identified
* Current workarounds are understood
* The problem is meaningful, not trivial
* The focus remains on the problem, not the solution
Revision Required If
* The problem is vague or abstract
* The solution dominates the narrative
* Users are poorly defined
* The problem is purely hypothetical
* There is no evidence the problem exists


Outcomes of Phase 0
After review, one of the following will happen:
1. Approved
You may proceed to Phase 1 (Business Viability Stress Test)
2. Approved with Revision
Minor clarification needed before Phase 1
3. Not Approved (Reframe Required)
You must rethink the problem or choose a new one
This is normal and not penalized.
Why Phase 0 Matters
Teams that skip or rush Phase 0 typically:
   * Build products nobody wants
   * Force AI into weak problems
   * Struggle to justify business logic later
   * Overbuild MVPs that collapse in later phases
Phase 0 prevents these failures.
How Phase 0 Connects to the Course Project
   * Phase 1 tests whether this problem can survive as a business
   * Phase 2 replaces assumptions with evidence
   * Phase 3 builds only what matters
   * Phase 4 determines whether the product deserves to scale
Final Note to Students
A good product starts with a problem you can explain without slides.
If you cannot explain the problem clearly, you are not ready to build.
