AI Product Development: Course Project — Phase 2
Research Report: Product, Business, and Market Evidence
Purpose of Phase 2
Phase 2 exists to replace assumptions with evidence.
In Phase 1, you identified risks, gaps, and weak assumptions in your product idea. In Phase 2, you will conduct structured research to either:
* Validate those assumptions with evidence, or
* Discover that the idea must be narrowed, repositioned, or pivoted
This phase ensures that when you build in Phase 3, you are building with intentional scope and informed trade-offs, not guesses.
Submission Requirements
You must submit a research report (maximum 4 A4 pages, excluding references) that investigates the critical foundations of your course project across three layers:
1. Product Research
2. Business Research 
3. Market / Industry Research 
4. Economic Research (Macro-Raisks)
Required Structure
Your report must contain all sections below.


1. Product Research (Critical)
Key question:
What exactly must this product do to solve the problem meaningfully?
Key consideration:
We have LMC (lean model canvas) already built -> we will do the research for validating Product Viability?
You must address:
A. User Needs & Pain Points
* Who are the users?
* What are they trying to achieve?
* Where do they struggle today?
* Evidence sources may include:
   * User interviews
   * Surveys
   * Public complaints, forums, reviews
   * Case studies or reports
B. Existing Solutions & Substitutes
* What tools, workflows, or workarounds are used today?
* What do these solutions do well?
* Where do they fail or create friction?
C. Product Scope & Differentiation
* What must your product do to deliver value?
* What will it explicitly not attempt to solve?
* How is your approach meaningfully different (based on evidence)?
Avoid architecture and implementation details.
2. Business Research
Key question:
Why could this exist as a sustainable product or business?
Key consideration:
We have BMC (business model canvas) and Value Chains already built -> we will do the research for validating Business Viability?
You must address:
A. Customer & Buyer Dynamics
* Who uses the product vs who pays?
* How are purchasing decisions made?
* What incentives or constraints exist?
B. Revenue & Cost Logic (High-Level)
* How similar products are priced
* Willingness-to-pay evidence or proxies
* Primary cost drivers (development, data, maintenance, operations)
No financial model required, but reasoning must be credible.
C. Business Risks & Constraints
* Operational risks
* Trust, compliance, or governance concerns
* Scaling limitations or dependencies
3. Market / Industry Research (Critical)
Key question:
Why does this problem matter in the broader market, and why now?
Key consideration:
We need to decide whether we will conduct Primary Research (new knowledge) or Secondary Research (existing knowledge and trends) or both?
You must address:
A. Market Context
* Target market definition
* Market size or proxy indicators
* Relevant industry trends
B. Competitive Landscape
* Direct competitors
* Indirect competitors
* Non-consumption or manual alternatives
Explain:
* Who wins today
* Why they win
* Where they leave gaps
C. Structural Barriers
* Switching costs
* Regulatory or legal constraints
* Organizational or cultural resistance
3. Economic Research (Critical)
Key question:
Why does this problem matter in the economic market currently?
Key consideration:
We need to decide the macro risks including inflation, politics, regulations and other risks?
Assumptions & Evidence
Throughout the report, you must:
* Explicitly label assumptions
* Identify which assumptions are now supported by evidence
* Highlight assumptions that remain high-risk
A strong report makes uncertainty visible.


Phase 2 Summary Section 
End your report with one page or less that answers:
* What assumptions were confirmed?
* What assumptions remain risky?
* How has your product scope changed?
* What will your MVP in Phase 3 focus on and exclude?

Common Mistakes to Avoid
* Writing a literature review instead of applied research
* Ignoring evidence that contradicts your idea
* Overusing buzzwords without support
* Treating assumptions as facts
* Including technical design prematurely
* Expanding scope instead of narrowing it
Outcomes of Phase 2
After review, you should have:
* A justified product scope
* Evidence-backed user understanding
* Reduced business and market uncertainty
* A clear plan for what the MVP must test
